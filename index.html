The Impact of Weather on Australia's Economy

The changing weather patterns in Australia have had a profound influence on the nation’s economy, affecting industries such as agriculture, livestock, and even skin health.

The following visualisations explore how weather and climate have influenced Australia’s economy over time.

Average Monthly and Annual Maximum Temperatures in Australia

This chart shows the average monthly and annual maximum temperatures across Australia. Use the dropdown to explore temperature variations by month.
As you can see, the temperature tends to rise as we move from south to north. We will be exploring how these temperature changes impact various sectors of Australia's economy.

Total Fire Area in Australia

The total fire area represents the amount of area in which bushfires have covered in Australia in a year. Increasing trends may indicate rising fire risks due to hotter, drier conditions that amplify the likelihood of bushfires occurring. 2015-2016 saw the highest total fire area, coinciding with a severe El Niño event that brought hotter and drier conditions to much of Australia. This included 4 notable bushfires that impacted Western Australia, South Australia, and Victoria.

The Impact of Drought on Water Usage in Australia

This captures the change in water usage over time between different water sources during the 2017-2019 drought. The only water source that saw an increase in usage in this period was groundwater, likely due to reduced surface water availability from the drought which would've dried up rivers and reservoirs that are commonly used for water supply.

Customer Price Index (CPI) is a metric used to measure the average change over time in the prices paid by consumers for goods and services. CPI starts at 100 in the base year and a CPI of 150 indicates that prices have increased by 50% since the base year.

Cyclone Yasi and Banana Prices in Australia

Cyclone Yasi (2011) landed in Queensland and caused a lot of destruction to the environment. This visualisation explores how Cyclone Yasi impacted banana prices in Australia. The sudden spike in the Customer Price Index (CPI) for bananas after the landing of the cyclone indicates a significant increase in banana prices, likely due to the disruption in supply caused by the cyclone. Several reasons include damage to banana crops which reduced supply, increased transportation costs due to damaged infrastructure as well as increased customer demands due to the shortage of bananas. Even after the immediate aftermath of Cyclone Yasi, banana prices remained very high for almost a year implying the devastating impacts of natural disasters on agricultural produce and prices.

Drought and its Impact on Livestock Revenue

After the drought in 2019, the revenue from livestock and livestock products became very volatile. This is likely due to the reduced availability of water and feed for livestock during drought conditions, resulting in reduced producer confidence where they felt the need to supply more livestock to the processors to offset losses from drought conditions. This has also affected wool revenue as sheep farmers may have had to sell off their sheep earlier than planned and also a decrease in the wool's quality from the reduced availability of water and feed. To examine the trends more closely, you can filter to select a specific export type.

How is the Sun affecting Australians' Skin?

This explores the percentage of Australians who have been sunburnt and those who have not been sunburnt. Use the dropdown menu to view different State/Territory's percentages.

Surprisingly, Tasmania has the highest percentage of sunburnt individuals despite it having the lowest average maximum temperature out of all the states/territories. This could be due to several factors such as sun protection habits and high UV levels during summer months that Tasmanians may not be aware of due to the generally cooler climate. As expected the next highest percentages of sunburnt individuals are in the warmer states/territories such as Queensland, Northern Territory and Western Australia where people are more exposed to higher temperatures and UV levels that are apparent in these regions.
